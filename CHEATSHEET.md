# Flow Metrics & Monte Carlo Cheatsheet

A quick reference for Scrum Masters explaining probabilistic forecasting to executives.

## The 4 Core Flow Metrics (Vacanti Canon)
1. **Work In Progress (WIP):** The count of items actively being worked on.
2. **Cycle Time:** Total elapsed time from an item starting to an item finishing.
3. **Throughput:** The exact count of items moved to "Done" per timeframe (replaces Velocity).
4. **Work Item Age:** Elapsed time since an active item started (the ultimate leading indicator of risk).

## Forecast Types
* **"When" Forecast:** Simulates the calendar date a *fixed scope* (like an Epic) will be completed.
* **"How Many" Forecast:** Simulates the number of items a team can deliver before a *fixed date* (like a hard release deadline).

## Chart Analysis
* **The S-Curve:** The cumulative probability line indicating the mathematical certainty of delivery climbing from 0% to 100% over time.
* **50th Percentile:** A coin toss. Planning for this date guarantees failure half the time. Do not commit here.
* **85th Percentile:** The industry standard. Indicates your team met this deadline in 8,500 out of 10,000 simulated futures.
* **Narrow Distribution:** The histogram bars are tight. Indicates steady, predictable team throughput.
* **Wide Distribution:** The histogram bars are spread far apart. Indicates erratic delivery; the forecast is highly sensitive and risky.
